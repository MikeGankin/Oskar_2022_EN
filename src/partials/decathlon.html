decathlon

In addition to the philosophy of Action, Oskar Hartmann defines «decathlon» as a derivative of the meaning of life — a principle that he invented for himself. Oskar is convinced that a person should not throw all his strength into just one area: business, career, sports or family. Then the other aspects of life will remain «overboard». A well-off person will be sick and lonely, and a successful athlete will be left without means of livelihood.

Harmony in a  person’s life, according to Hartmann, appears when a person equally develops a set of directions. The entrepreneur is  convinced that it is possible to be a truly active person, being in constant active movement towards his dreams, and at the same time be in balance and harmony with himself. Oskar shows by his example that with great diligence this is quite feasible. «If you can effectively dose all the components of  your life — family, career, relationships, health and hobbies  — you will get the most beautiful composition of your life»— says Hartmann.

Paying great attention not only to business, but also to sports, creativity, and family, the entrepreneur has identified for himself ten disciplines in which he tries to succeed:

  • A healthy, energetic, life-loving person
  • Husband
  • Father
  • Son, brother, part of his family
  • Friend and partner
  • Businessman
  • Investor
  • Philanthropist with a focus on entrepreneurship development
  • Athlete, sprinter, rower
  • A spiritual person with a hobby
